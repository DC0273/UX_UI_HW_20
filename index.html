NPR UX Redesign

As a class project to redesign the popular radio broadcast, npr. First we thing back to when newspapers were still around, how many of us would skip straight to the sports section or the weekly funnies, etc? Many of us have and would rather go straight to the sections we have the most interest in. So we want to implement a new personalized experience based on the user’s interests, and add features to help cross promote npr’s other great content.

My Role:UX Designer, User Research

Research

We start with heuristic evaluation and we found the experience of npr’s site quite pleasant from a UX/UI start point with minor issues. But, what does the general public think? We discovered through usability tests that the current site is actually very intuitive and people found it clean and simple. So we decided to ask broader questions, to understand how the general public prefers to view their news, and how can we improve their news consumption. But first we must under stand the needs of the stakeholders.

“NPR must expand its audience and ensure that it’s journalism be more reflective of a diverse American public (a goal it has openly declared one of its highest priorities.)”

-Kelly McBride, NPR’s New Public Editor

“We know that listeners are-by far- our most engaged audience. They spend far more time with us and are much more loyal than readers.”

-Dan Newman, NPR’s Director of Product Design

“If I could focus on one thing, there’s still room for improvement in cross promoting. The thing could do a better job at is surfacing the rest of what we do.”

-Dan Newman, NPR’s Director of Product Design

Affinity Diagram shows that many demographics use npr for a specific purpose.

Summary of Problem

  • Not enough exploration of other content (NPR would like to showcase archived material more).
  • Users are shown irrelevant content, they would rather not read.
  • Means to diversify their (NPR’s) audience

User Persona

Ideation:

Add; Recommended, Listen, Popular, Latest tabs to the home page. These sections currently don’t exist with npr.



Reseasoning for adding new features:

A recommended tab will cater the content to the user’s interests. This is also a great way to cross promote nrp’s other material people don’t get a chance to see. By creating a personalized feed based on the individual will help diversity the audience. Essentially “killing 2 birds with 1 stone.” In this case, 3 birds with 1 stone. Furthermore, adding a Listen Tab will cater to npr’s most engaged audience by showing all the listening content. And finally, a Popular and Latest tabs for those users who just want to get caught up on the news. I believe by adding new features for the redesign will create a lasting and deeper relationship for all users while increasing the time they spend with us and creating multiple touch points.

Validating Proposed Resolution

I felt I had a very solid idea. So I sought to validate that these resolution methods would appeal to users. Asking, “what kind of news content do you want to see?” Ended up with an overwhelmingly positive response, validating my claim.

Testing

Task 1

Goal/Output: Quickly spot the home/home navigation and understand the options.

Assumptions: User will look at the top for the navigation, and read the options.

Steps: Look to the top

Success Criteria: User will find navigation at glance and easily understand the options.

Notes: Ask them to find the navigation menu. Do they understand the navigation menu, at what point did you realize what the different nav items meant

Task 2

Goal/Output: Quickly spot the home/home navigation and understand the options.

Assumptions: User will get confused at first and hopefully figure out that it changed because they selected a new section

Steps: Go to a section from the hamburger menu, the navigation bar will change, with a new set of navigation options.

Success Criteria: User does not get bothered or confused by the new set of navigation options.

Notes: Will they understand that when they selected the new option, these are the navigation menu for this specific section.

Task 3

Goal/Output: User get back to the home page, with no issues.

Assumptions: User will tap on the logo

Steps:In a different section with nav bar options changed to the specific section. Tap on npr logo to get back to home page.

Success Criteria: User quickly understands that the logo is the home button.

Notes: Will they go for the logo, or will they look for a home icon or button?
